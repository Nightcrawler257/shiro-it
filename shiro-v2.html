PC Care

Keep Your PC Healthy

Clean Your PC Regularly

Dust buildup causes overheating. Use compressed air to clean your fans, heatsinks, and filters every 3-6 months. Keep vents unblocked for proper airflow.

MaintenanceHardware

Keep Drivers Updated

Outdated GPU, chipset, and network drivers cause crashes and poor performance. Update drivers monthly from the manufacturer's website — avoid third-party tools.

PerformanceSoftware

Monitor Temperatures

Use HWMonitor or Core Temp to check CPU/GPU temps. CPU should stay below 85°C under load. If temps are high, repaste thermal compound or improve case airflow.

HardwarePerformance

Enable Windows Defender

Windows Defender is free and effective. Keep it enabled with real-time protection on. Avoid downloading cracked software — it's the #1 source of malware.

SecuritySoftware

Upgrade to SSD

Still on a hard drive? An SSD is the single biggest upgrade for speed. Windows boots in seconds, apps open instantly. NVMe SSDs are even faster than SATA SSDs.

HardwareUpgrade

Backup Your Data

Follow the 3-2-1 rule: 3 copies of data, on 2 different media, with 1 offsite (cloud). Use Google Drive, OneDrive, or an external drive. Don't wait until it's too late!

DataSecurity

Optimize Your WiFi

Place your router centrally and elevated. Use 5GHz band for speed near the router, 2.4GHz for range. Change default passwords and use WPA3 encryption.

NetworkingSecurity

Check RAM Usage

Open Task Manager (Ctrl+Shift+Esc) to check memory usage. If you're consistently above 80%, it's time to upgrade. 16GB is the sweet spot for most users in 2025.

PerformanceHardware

Use a Surge Protector

Power surges can fry your components instantly. Always use a surge protector or UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply) — especially in areas with unstable electricity.

HardwareSafety

Need Expert Help?

Our technicians can diagnose and fix any PC issue. From slow performance to hardware failures — we've got you covered.

Yes! All custom builds come with a full 2-year warranty on labor and individual component warranties from manufacturers.

Can I upgrade my existing PC?

Absolutely! We offer upgrade services for CPUs, GPUs, RAM, storage, and more. Bring your PC in for a free assessment.

Do you offer on-site services?

Yes, we provide on-site services within the Penang area for networking setups, PC repairs, and corporate IT support.

What payment methods do you accept?

We accept cash, bank transfer, online banking (FPX), and major credit/debit cards. Installment plans available.

Do you accept trade-ins?

Yes, we do! You can trade in your old PC components, laptops, or complete systems toward the purchase of new IT services or a custom PC build. Validations will be done upon inspection.
